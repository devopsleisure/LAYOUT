Key Concepts

📋

CPT Codes Overview

Current Procedural Terminology (CPT) codes describe medical services and procedures for billing and reimbursement. Each 5-digit code precisely identifies what was done to the patient, ensuring payers reimburse correctly for every service rendered.

🔌

ICD-10 Diagnosis Coding

ICD-10-CM codes capture diagnoses and clinical conditions to justify treatment, support claims, and maintain compliance. Accurate diagnosis coding is critical for medical necessity documentation and prevents costly claim denials.

📄

Insurance Claim Workflow

Verify eligibility at scheduling, assign accurate codes, submit clean claims via clearinghouse, monitor payer adjudication, post payments, and resolve any denials with timely appeals and supporting documentation.

🔄

Revenue Cycle Stages

Scheduling, registration, charge capture, coding, claim filing, adjudication, payment posting, patient statements, and AR follow-up — each stage directly impacts cash flow, days in AR, and overall practice financial health.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Revenue Cycle Management?
Revenue Cycle Management (RCM) covers all financial activities from patient registration and scheduling through coding, claims submission, payer adjudication, payment posting, and final payment collection. Effective RCM maximizes reimbursements, minimizes denials, and keeps cash flow healthy for healthcare providers.
What is AR Follow-Up?
Accounts Receivable Follow-Up means actively reviewing unpaid claims, tracking their status with payers, identifying delays, and coordinating resolution to secure timely payment. Regular AR follow-up prevents revenue from aging out and ensures no claim falls through the cracks.
What causes claim denials?
Common denial causes include coding errors (wrong CPT or ICD-10 codes), missing or incomplete documentation, eligibility issues, missing prior authorization, duplicate submissions, and filing deadline misses. Most denials are preventable with strong front-end verification and clean claim workflows.
What is the difference between CPT and ICD-10?
CPT (Current Procedural Terminology) codes represent the services and procedures performed — what was done to the patient. ICD-10-CM codes represent diagnoses and clinical conditions — why the service was performed. Both must be accurate and correctly linked for a claim to be paid.
How do you reduce days in AR?
Reducing AR days requires pre-submission claim scrubbing, real-time eligibility verification, systematic payer follow-up cadences, denial root-cause analysis, and strong appeals workflows. Through these methods, I reduced average AR days from 52 to 28 — a 46% improvement — at a multi-physician practice.
